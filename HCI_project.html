Project Description

Single-use takeaway containers contribute significantly to environmental waste and carbon emissions. Despite increased awareness, most consumers lack convenient alternatives to reduce their reliance on disposable containers. This project utilized the double diamond design process to create a solution focusing on reducing waste.

Phase 1: Discover

Through in-depth interviews with frequent food delivery users and restaurant staff, we explored their experiences and attitudes toward takeaway packaging. This was complemented by research on sustainability trends and reusable container models to identify best practices and emerging solutions. The findings showed that while users express genuine interest in sustainable packaging options, convenience and ease of use remain the main factors influencing their willingness to move away from single-use packaging.

Phase 2: Define

We defined our target users as people seeking convenient meal solutions and restaurants aiming to reduce waste while appealing to eco-conscious customers. Based on their needs, we identified key features for the app: reusable boxes that users can borrow and return by scanning QR codes, and a restaurant integration system that simplifies logistics by allowing restaurants to clean and manage the boxes efficiently.

Phase 3: develop

We explored a range of ideas through brainstorming, including subscription models, deposit systems, and digital tracking for reusable containers. From these concepts, we moved into prototyping and created high-fidelity wireframes to visualize the user flow. The prototype focused on enabling users to discover nearby partner restaurants, browse menu items available in reusable containers, and track their personal environmental impact.

Phase 4: Deliver

we conducted A/B testing to evaluate and refine the app’s design and functionality. The goal was to identify which design elements and messaging best encouraged user engagement and trust. Variant A emphasized environmental impact metrics, such as “You saved 20 containers,” while Variant B focused on convenience by highlighting nearby restaurants and quick ordering options. The results showed that users preferred a balance between visible impact and easy restaurant access, leading to the development of a hybrid home screen layout that incorporated both elements.
